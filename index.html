notes

on learning slowly

a reflection on what it actually means to absorb something versus just knowing about it. there's a difference, and i don't think we talk about it enough.

  • understanding ≠ memorizing
  • the most useful knowledge lives in the gaps between lessons
  • going slow is often the faster path
  • boredom is information

the uncomfortable middle

every project has a phase where nothing works and you can't see the end. this is about what that feels like and why it matters that you sit in it anyway.

  • most people quit here — that's the advantage
  • the middle is where your taste starts to show
  • it looks like failure from the outside

systems over willpower

willpower runs out. structure doesn't care how you feel. some notes on how i've been thinking about building habits that survive bad weeks.

  • remove friction more than you add motivation
  • good defaults carry you when discipline fails
  • small loops compound silently
  • the environment shapes more than the intention

about

i'm someone who thinks too much and builds to process it. this channel is how i make sense of things — businesses, systems, ideas that feel obvious in retrospect but weren't when i started.

i'm not an expert. i'm figuring it out in real time, which means sometimes i'm wrong, and sometimes i change my mind. i think that's more honest than pretending to have answers i don't have.

most of what i make starts as a question i couldn't stop thinking about. i'm interested in how things actually work — not the polished version, but the version with the rough edges still on it.

location san diego, california
doing college, card business, building things slowly
interested in systems, markets, the process behind the result
